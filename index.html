MedSeaFish Explorer: Dive into Mediterranean Seagrass-Fish Data

MedSeaFishE (Mediterranean Seagrass Fish Data Explorer) is an interactive database offering a comprehensive, up-to-date catalogue of fish species observed in Mediterranean seagrass habitats. Drawing on decades of research, it lets users explore which species have been reported in association with different seagrass types, life stages, sampling methods, seasons, and more. Through a systematic review and a newly built Mediterranean-wide dataset, MedSeaFishE offers the first comprehensive picture of fish species associated with native and invasive seagrass meadows, supporting the protection of biodiversity and the sustainable future of Mediterranean marine ecosystems.

Inside the project

Seagrasses are essential habitats in the Mediterranean Sea, one of the worldâ€™s major biodiversity hotspots. They provide food, shelter, and nursery grounds for a wide variety of fish species, from small herbivores to large predators, while indirectly sustaining fisheries of high commercial and social value. By hosting species at different life stages, seagrass meadows play a critical role in maintaining marine biodiversity and ecosystem functioning.

Despite decades of research, most studies on Mediterranean seagrasses have been carried out at local scales, often with different methods and objectives, making it difficult to build a clear, basin-wide picture of how fish communities use these habitats. At the same time, seagrass ecosystems face multiple pressures, including habitat loss, climate change, and the spread of invasive species such as Halophila stipulacea, which is rapidly expanding and reshaping native fishâ€“habitat relationships. Understanding how native and invasive seagrasses differ in supporting fish communities is therefore crucial for both biodiversity conservation and fisheries management.

To address this gap, we performed a systematic review using a semi-automated filtering approach to compile all available information on fish species observed in Mediterranean seagrass habitats. The resulting dataset provides, for the first time, a comprehensive overview of fish diversity across the basin.

Future development will include ecological metadata and fish functional traits linked to seagrass use, in a continuous effort to fill knowledge gaps for future research, including the urgent need to evaluate the ecological consequences of invasive seagrasses on Mediterranean fish communities.

To compile the dataset of fish species associated with Mediterranean seagrasses, we followed the nine steps of the JBI methodology and PRISMA guidelines, adapting the protocol to handle the very large number of publications retrieved.

01. Identification

1. Searches on six bibliographic databases (Dimension, Mendeley, ScienceDirect, Scopus, Web of Science, Wiley Online Library).
2. 69 keywords across three main fields (Mediterranean, seagrass, fish), combined with Boolean operators.
3. No restrictions on publication year.
4. Duplicates and incomplete records removed.

02. Screening

1. Papers scored using 193 key-terms across four categories:
     i) Seagrasses habitat
     ii) Fish biology
     iii) Sampling methods and assemblages
     iv) Geography.
2. A Relevance Score (RS) calculated for each paper.
3. Sequential filtering based on:
    i) Minimum threshold for relevance
    ii) Removal of papers with low RS
    iii) Exclusion based on most frequent and negative keywords

03. Eligibility 

1. Abstracts screened by expert team.
2. Backward and forward reference searches performed to capture missing studies.
3. Data manually extracted for each fish species, including detailed metadata (ecoregion, FAO zone, site, habitat, life stage, sampling method, season, year, coordinates, bibliographic source).
4. Singletons (species recorded in only one study) have been excluded, except for Lessepsian species.

How to use

 
Choose your filters

Select a seagrass species (e.g. Posidonia oceanica, Cymodocea nodosa). Narrow results by fish species, life stage, season, substrate, or sampling method.

Explore the results

View a table of fish records matching your selection. Use the map to see where species have been observed across the Mediterranean.

Download the data

Export filtered datasets for your own research and analysis.

Discover insights

Compare how different seagrass habitats support fish diversity. Identify gaps in knowledge, such as under-represented regions or life stages.
